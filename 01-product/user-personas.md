# Lexio User Personas (MVP)

## Primary: "Career-Driven Carla"

- **Demographics**: 28, São Paulo, PT-BR native, intermediate English (B1)
- **Tech comfort**: High (uses Notion, Duolingo, LinkedIn daily)
- **Motivation**: Career advancement (multinational job, promotions, salary negotiation in English)
- **Daily routine**: Commutes 1h each way on metro — mobile-first consumption
- **Pain points with current apps**:
  - "They ignore how the Portuguese brain works"
  - "No real grammar explanations, just memorization"
  - "Feels like homework, not mental structure building"
  - "Duolingo gamifies the wrong things"
- **Lexio hook**: "Finally, an app that explains WHY English works differently"
- **Retention driver**: Career progress visibility — "You're 73% to B2. That's your next salary negotiation."

## Secondary: "Student Pedro"

- **Demographics**: 22, Rio, PT-BR native, pre-intermediate English (A2)
- **Tech comfort**: High (TikTok, Discord, mobile-first)
- **Motivation**: Study abroad scholarship, academic reading, international network
- **Pain points**: "Apps are too childish or too academic — nothing in between"
- **Lexio hook**: "Quirky but serious — matches my vibe"
- **Retention driver**: Streak gamification + Leo as a study buddy

## Excluded from MVP

| Group | Reason |
|-------|--------|
| PT-PT speakers | Focus PT-BR first; lexical/phonological differences require separate corpus |
| Beginners below A2 | Placement quiz filters them — insufficient comprehensible input base |
| Non-career/academic learners | Niche expansion post-PMF |
| Native English-adjacent speakers | No PT-BR interference patterns to address |

## Shared Characteristics (Both Personas)

- Tried Duolingo, dropped off within 30 days
- Smartphone as primary computing device
- Respond better to "why" explanations than rote repetition
- Willing to pay if they see structural progress (not just XP)
- Brazilian cultural references land better than US-centric examples
