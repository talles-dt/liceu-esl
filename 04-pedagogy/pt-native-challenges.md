# PT-BR Native Learning Challenges (PT-BR → English)

## Top 5 Interference Points (MVP Focus)

These are the highest-frequency structural errors made by PT-BR speakers. Every Grammar pillar lesson should address at least one.

| Error Pattern | PT-BR Source | English Incorrect | English Correct | Lexio Strategy |
|--------------|-------------|------------------|----------------|----------------|
| **Age expression** | "Tenho 25 anos" (ter = to have) | "I have 25 years" | "I am 25 years old" | [GRAMMAR] Contrast 'ter' vs 'be'. [MNEMONIC] Birthday cake with 'AM' candles |
| **Subject pronoun dropping** | "Estou cansado" (subject optional) | "Is raining" | "It is raining" | [GRAMMAR] English forces explicit subject. [LOGIC] Reduces ambiguity in fast speech |
| **Temporal conjugation** | "Ontem eu vou ao mercado" (present acceptable) | "I go yesterday" | "I went yesterday" | [GRAMMAR] PT-BR more flexible; English past = strict past tense |
| **Adjective order** | "carro vermelho grande" (flexible) | "car red big" | "big red car" | [GRAMMAR] English locks order: opinion→size→age→color→noun |
| **Question word inversion** | "Você vai onde?" (word-final OK) | "You are going where?" | "Where are you going?" | [GRAMMAR] English inverts subject+verb after question word |

## Secondary Interference Points (Post-MVP)

- **False cognates**: "actually" ≠ "atualmente" / "embarrassed" ≠ "embaraçada"
- **Gerund vs infinitive**: "I enjoy to swim" → "I enjoy swimming"
- **Preposition mismatch**: "married with" → "married to"
- **Definite article overuse**: "The life is beautiful" → "Life is beautiful"
- **Modal register**: "Can you help me?" vs "Could you help me?" — register distinction missing in PT-BR

## Correction Framework (3-Layer)

Every PT-BR interference correction MUST include all three layers:

1. **Explicit warning**: "PT-BR speakers often [error]. Instead, English requires [correct pattern]."
2. **Logic explanation**: "This structure exists because [cognitive or cultural reason]."
3. **Memory anchor**: Mnemonic linking the PT-BR mental structure to the English pattern.

## Krashen-Aligned Scaffolding for PT-BR Learners

- Begin with high-cognate vocabulary to lower affective filter (e.g., "information", "natural", "possible")
- Introduce structural contrasts gradually: 1 per lesson maximum
- Use memory palaces anchored in Brazilian cultural references (São Paulo metro, café da manhã, futebol, reunião)
- Allow brief PT-BR code-switching in early lessons (A2) when introducing new structures
- Never shame the error — always frame as discovery: "This is where Portuguese and English think differently"
